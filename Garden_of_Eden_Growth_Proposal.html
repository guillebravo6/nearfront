SEO growth, phased across 12 months
Phase 1 · Now

SEO: foundation

Fix the GBP delivery category gap on both listings, rebuild the delivery landing page around "dispensary delivery near me" and "dispensary delivery," bring Tracy's on-site content and links up to Hayward's standard, and launch geo-expansion pages for Sunol, Livermore, and Mountain House ahead of or alongside their delivery buildout.

Backed by a structured authority and AI-visibility program: interlinked social profiles and web 2.0 properties, tiered drip-feed indexing, multiple authority hubs with citations, and contextual, high-authority backlinks to the homepage first, roughly 20 links to pave the way for every dispensary and delivery page as it publishes. The first three months focus on overall site authority plus Tracy's organic ranking and AI surfacing, with the goal of overtaking Stiiizy in Tracy's search results.

Programmatic retargeting launches in parallel from month 1, a small, dedicated budget aimed at the existing customer list and site visitors rather than cold acquisition, so there is a return-generating channel live from day one while SEO compounds underneath it.

Phase 2 · Month 4–6

SEO: expansion

Geo-expansion pages extend to the next tier of delivery cities. The backlink program moves past the homepage to the delivery hub page and any city page outside the top 5. Tracy's organic ranking and Domain Rating climb toward overtaking Stiiizy in Tracy's search results, and AI surfacing improves alongside it. Programmatic retargeting continues at the same lean budget, reinvesting returns rather than scaling media spend.

Phase 3 · Month 7–12

SEO: maturity

Full city-page coverage across both delivery zones, Domain Rating matured off a year of contextual link building, and organic search carrying the majority of incremental revenue growth. Programmatic retargeting is reviewed against a full year of data and rebudgeted only if the existing-customer list has grown enough to justify it.

Data and methodology: Revenue baseline and all Treez figures are Q2 2026 (April–June) actuals pulled directly from Treez POS exports, confirmed by location. Google Business Profile, Search Console, and Local Falcon figures are live via Windsor.ai and Local Falcon's API as of July 20, 2026. Google Analytics 4 is connected and confirms 49,824 sessions for the quarter, but purchase and transaction tracking is not yet implemented in GA4, so this model is built on Treez POS actuals rather than GA4 revenue attribution; installing that tracking is a parallel recommendation, not a dependency for this plan. The Ahrefs Rank Tracker project is now fully populated with all 50 keywords across the 25 cities in the confirmed delivery zone, including the five most recently added (Oakley, Brentwood, Wagner, Atlanta, and Knightsen), and the keyword-level proof above reflects live data across the full set. This proposal reflects our full engagement tier: Garden of Eden has granted Google tools access plus point-of-sale and loyalty system access, which is what makes a model like this possible in the first place. A lighter, no-access review only covers what's publicly visible; this one is built on the actual numbers. This plan intentionally excludes paid Google Ads and Meta acquisition spend. The same 6- and 12-month revenue targets are carried by SEO plus a lean, existing-customer retargeting program, which keeps total monthly investment lower while targeting a materially higher blended return than a paid-acquisition-led plan would.
